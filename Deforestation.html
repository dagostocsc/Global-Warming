Deforestation

What is Deforestation?

Deforestation is the permanent removal of trees to make room for something besides forest. This can include clearing the land for agriculture or grazing, or using the timber for fuel, construction, or manufacturing.

Why is Deforestation important?

Deforestation is important to understand because forests play a vital role in maintaining Earth's balance. They absorb carbon dioxide, regulate the climate, protect biodiversity, and maintain the water cycle. Forests also prevent soil erosion, reduce the risk of natural disasters like floods and droughts, and support the livelihoods of over a billion people, especially Indigenous communities. When we destroy forests, we are not just losing trees, we are dismantling the systems that keep our planet stable.

Why should we care?

We should care about deforestation because its impacts reach far beyond the areas where trees are cut. It contributes significantly to climate change, accelerates species extinction, increases food and water insecurity, and raises the risk of diseases spreading from animals to humans. The loss of forests threatens our environment, our health, and our future. Protecting forests means protecting life on Earth, including our own.
